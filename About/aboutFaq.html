All of Your Questions

1. What's the best diet for weight loss?
Any diet you can stick with for as long as it's going to take. Studies have shown that regardless of the diet followed, adherence is the only thing that predicts success. Beware of any diet that promises rapid weight loss (and expects you to consume fewer than 1000 calories per day).

2. What should I eat after a workout?
you should eat a small snack consisting of protein and easily digested carbohydrates within about an hour of training and then your next meal an hour or two later.

3. Which should I do first; cardio or weights?
There's some evidence suggesting that if you're doing both in a single session "weights before cardio" leads to faster fat loss, for most people the outcome will be the same regardless of which they do first.

4. What should I eat before a workout?
you should have some form of fuel in your system before you work out. Eating an easily digested carbohydrate an hour or so before you hit the gym ensures that you'll have enough energy in the tank to get through your program.

5. How quickly will I see the results of my training?
Expect to FEEL the results of your training sooner than you SEE them. People who start a new exercise program and are consistent in getting their workouts done typically report improvements in sleep, mood and energy levels within two to three weeks.

6. Why can't I just do cardio?
While cardiovascular training is great for building strong hearts and lungs, it doesn't provide the stimulus your body needs to build bigger, stronger muscles and bones.
